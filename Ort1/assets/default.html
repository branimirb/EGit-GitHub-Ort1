Main Screen & More Readouts Screen

You may choose the readouts for the three sections of these screens by performing a long-press on a specific section. After pressing the Menu button and going to "Settings", if you disable the option "Lock portrait screen orientation for readouts screens" the first of three readouts will take up the whole screen in case the device is put in the horizontal orientation. Long press back button for returning to Main Screen.

Race Timer Screen

By pressing gear icon in bottom right corner of a screen, you may change initial time to race, or choose use of multiclass mode. In handicap mode you may change time offset between two classes. After starting a race this settings is not available.

Gestures

The user is able to navigate through the circle of screens by performing a swipe (fling) gesture across the screen to the left or to the right.
A Z Gesture (its path resembles the capital letter "Z") is used for locking and unlocking the screen.

Buoys

The start line of a race is a line from Pin (its port side) to the Race Committee (its starboard side). Other than that are also the three markers and the finish line.

The Buoys screen enables you to record the coordinates of the buoys - either by coming to their location (long-press a buoy) or by recording two directions to the buoy from two different locations (press "Direction To" button) - the program will compute their intersection and save it as the buoy's position. Recording these directions is an action very similar to recording wind direction by sailing into it.

Recording True Wind Direction by Sailing Into It

Go to the True Wind Direction screen. Then press "Record" to memorize bearings received from the GPS while sailing directly into the wind. After clicking on "Stop", the program will offer you an average value of the bearings recorded which you may fine-tune using the "+" and "-" buttons. You may also use the "Use compass" button which will swap the current value with the current heading provided by the compass.

Program's first guess at the wind direction is the start line's centerline.

Tracks

The path recorded since the application started to the moment the user leaves the application is referred to as a track. The user can reset the track's beginning on the Stats screen.
A "Stopwatch Track" presents the path made while the stopwatch was on, and a "GPX Track" is a track loaded from a .gpx file.

Choosing the Tracks option inside the Menu will show a list of tracks. Clicking on a track inside the list will present a map that shows the complete recorded path. Long-press will provide the user with a list of options for the selected track (one of them being reviewing its legs and tacks).

Legs and Tacks

A leg is a part of the track that can be between markers or between the start/finish line and a marker.
Each leg has segments that show one approximately steady course - tacks! Statistics referring to tacks will be displayed in an expandable list for each leg.

Recording True Wind Direction as a Centerline of Two Directions

After clicking on this button button on the True Wind Direction screen, the program will automatically note the directions of the next two tacks and compute the true wind direction as their centerline. (This button will turn yellow after the first tack and turn green and display the wind direction value after the second one).

Measuring Currents

The idea is to throw a pin in the water and press "Record". Than you wait for the current to move it and once you get to the pin's new position, press "Stop". The program will then compute the velocity of the current and display it.

Rounding modes

You can choose between two modes for buoy rounding.
ASAP mode will detect incoming buoy as soon as possible while you are approaching it. It means that application will detect buoy rounding before physical rounding happens.
ALAP mode will detect buoy rounding after physical rounding happens. It means that application will detect buoy rounding while physical rounding is happening, but signal user when he starts to moving away from the buoy.
